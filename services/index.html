Services

End-to-End Energy Infrastructure Delivery

Source. Deploy. Operate. Three lifecycle pillars delivered under direct OEM technical authority — from factory documentation to sustained field integrity.

01 Source

Procurement and Supply

Viscera Energy sources directly from OEM agreements — not through intermediaries. Every valve, compressor, pump or generator set we supply comes with the factory documentation that makes it defensible in a FEED review, a pre-commissioning inspection, or a warranty claim.

Our OEM network spans thirty manufacturers across twelve countries — from Swedish valve makers to Swiss compressor specialists to Turkish generator OEMs. When we quote, we quote from the source. The factory documentation, material test certificates, and conformance certificates come with every supply — because they are non-negotiable in any serious energy project.

View Procurement Detail

What's Included

  • Valve and actuator packages — gate, globe, ball, butterfly, control, check, and specialty valves
  • Compressors, pumps and rotating equipment with OEM factory test reports
  • Line pipe, steel sections and structural consumables with full MTCs
  • HVAC systems and balancing valves for process and building services
  • Power generation equipment — diesel generating sets, solar integration packages
  • Instrumentation and metering — CTMS and PRMS packages
  • SONCAP and NCS-PAAR clearance documentation support
  • Factory Acceptance Test (FAT) coordination and third-party inspection
02 Deploy

Engineering and Integration

We provide engineering services through direct OEM technical partnerships and qualified engineering service providers — delivering the process design basis, P&IDs, and CTMS/PRMS packages that align equipment to field conditions.

Because we hold the OEM agreement, our engineering is not generic — it is grounded in the technical specifications, material standards, and application data of the manufacturers who made the equipment. This is the difference between an engineering report that holds up in a FEED review and one that does not. Our global engineering partners bring the process disciplines; we bring the OEM-to-field interface.

View Engineering Detail

Engineering Scope

  • Oil and gas facility engineering — fixed, floating, onshore and offshore applications
  • Pipeline and piping design to ASME B31.4 and B31.8 standards
  • CTMS and PRMS system design, specification and procurement
  • Modular and pre-fabricated unit design, specification and package management
  • EPC support and package coordination across multi-discipline teams
  • Technical manpower supply for project engineering phases
  • Procurement and supply chain management integration
  • OEM material supply and OEM-to-EPC interface management
03 Operate

Operations, Maintenance and Integrity

Our operations and maintenance capability is grounded in OEM technical authority. Because we hold the OEM agreement, we hold the factory training, spare parts access, and technical documentation that makes maintenance defensible.

This capability is actively expanding — anchored by the founder's two decades of operator-side experience and the OEM relationships that make it technically credible. The basis is sound: maintenance that cannot trace to factory documentation is maintenance that creates warranty exposure and regulatory risk. Ours can.

We do not pad this section with proposals presented as delivered work. Where capability is expanding, we say so.

O&M Capability

  • Rotating equipment operations and maintenance — compressors, pumps, generators

  • Static equipment inspection to API 510 and API 653
  • HVAC commissioning and ongoing maintenance programs
  • Field services and aftermarket support under OEM terms
